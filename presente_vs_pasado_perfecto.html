Presente Perfecto vs. Pasado Perfecto

Aspecto Presente Perfecto Pasado Perfecto
Estructura have/has + past participle
Forma larga: have / has
Forma corta: 've / 's		 had + past participle
Forma larga: had
Forma corta: 'd
Ejemplo (afirmativa) She has finished the report.
Forma larga: has
Forma corta: She's finished the report. 		She had finished the report before the meeting.
Forma larga: had
Forma corta: She'd finished the report before the meeting.
Ejemplo (negativa) They have not sent the email.
Forma larga: have not
Forma corta: They haven't sent the email. 		They had not sent the email.
Forma larga: had not
Forma corta: They hadn't sent the email.
Ejemplo (interrogativa) Have you checked the server?
Forma larga: have
Forma corta: — (no contracción al inicio de preguntas) 		Had you checked the server before 9 a.m.?
Forma larga: had
Forma corta: — (no contracción al inicio de preguntas)
Uso principal Acción pasada con relevancia en el presente Acción anterior a otra en el pasado
Referencia temporal already, yet, just, ever, never before, after, when, by the time
Conexión temporal Pasado ↠ Presente Pasado ↠ Pasado más lejano
Ejemplo con relación I have eaten (so I’m not hungry now).
Forma larga: have
Forma corta: I've eaten. 		I had eaten (before she arrived).
Forma larga: had
Forma corta: I'd eaten.
Traducción común He comido / hemos terminado Había comido / habían terminado
